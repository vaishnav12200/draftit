SC 06 — Factory Confrontation
INT. ABANDONED FACTORY — NIGHT
Rain hammers corrugated roofing. Rust-orange light bleeds through shattered skylights. The building breathes with the wind.
ARJUN (35, eyes that have forgotten how to sleep) picks through broken glass, single torch cutting the dark. Every shadow has a twin.
ARJUN
(barely a whisper)
She said you'd still be here.
A match-strike. The far corner ignites in amber. REZA (50s, the kind of calm that comes from crossing every line) sits on an overturned crate. He doesn't stand. He doesn't need to.
REZA
I never left. You're the one who's been running.
Arjun stops ten feet short. The torch trembles — or maybe his hand does.
ARJUN
(steadying himself)
Everything you told me about my father. Was any of it true?
Reza finally rises. He moves like weather — slow, inevitable, everywhere at once.
REZA
All of it was true. That's why you came here in the dark instead of the morning.
Arjun's jaw tightens. He reaches into his jacket —
